Discover Laramie

Demographics

Population

Laramie is the third largest city in Wyoming, after Cheyenne and Casper, with a population of 32,381.

Population by Age

Since Laramie is the home to the University of Wyoming, the University of Wyoming makes up about 12,450 of it's population and is a big contributor to Laramie's average age. Because of this the median age of Laramie is in the early 20's.

Median Individual Income

The median individual income is about 21.8K. As shown in the graph, this has been continually rising.

Education

With the University of Wyoming in Laramie, there is a big push for secondary education. About 4.9K people have completed a Bachelors degree.

This information was provided by Data Commons. Please visit for more information on Laramie.

Surrounded by Nature

Laramie is encircled by forests and mountains in all directions. This includes Medicine Bow National Forest, Snowy Range Mountains, Vedauwoo, Pole Mountain, Laramie Range, and Curt Gowdy State Park.

University of Wyoming

Laramie is home to the University of Wyoming, a publice land-grant research university. Along with academic and sporting events, the University of Wyoming is also home to a number of museums.

Outdoor Activities

Since Laramie is surrounded by forests and mountains, there are many wonderful hiking and biking trails.

Wyoming Territorial Prison State Historic Site

The Wyoming Territorial Prison was Wyoming's first State Penitentiary. Take a self-guided tour through the Historic site.

Jubilee Days

Come enjoy Laramie Jubilee Days July2-10 2022! This is a yearly celebration of Wyoming's Statehood.

Historic Laramie Union Pacific Train Depot

The Historic Laramie Union Pacific Train Depot, built in 1924, now houses the Laramie Historic Railroad Depot Museum.
